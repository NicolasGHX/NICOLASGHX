Useful Introduction

  • If you're a founder wanting to grow your startup, you're in the right place.
  • Angel Investors are wealthy individuals who invest personal money in early-stage companies.
  • They expect equity in return for their risky investment.
  • They are often family, friends, or business connections who believe in your vision.
  • This is the hardest stage of funding, as it’s your first and most challenging step.

The Premises: Your Project, Your Idea

  • What problem are you solving? Why does it matter?
  • Define the tangible solution you’re offering. Can it be implemented?
  • Is the timing right for your solution in the market?
  • Is your team prepared to handle early-stage challenges?
  • Identify barriers to market entry and strategize how to overcome them.
  • Understand your competition and how you will differentiate yourself.
  • Have a roadmap with specific funding requirements for each development stage.

Fundraising Outreach

  • Identify likely interested Angel Investors and personalize your outreach.
  • Build relationships through thoughtful emails or networking events.
  • Highlight common interests or shared goals when contacting investors.
  • Be patient and allow relationships to develop before making a formal pitch.
  • Even rejections can lead to valuable referrals, so always follow up politely.
  • Prepare your pitch in advance, as interest can lead to meetings quickly.

Pitching

  • Explain the problem and the market opportunity it creates.
  • Describe your solution and how it effectively addresses the problem.
  • Showcase your team’s qualifications, diversity, and capability to adapt.
  • Demonstrate yourself as a determined, adaptable, and visionary founder.
  • Include a product demo to leave a tangible impression on investors.
  • Keep your pitch concise and focused on key facts and value propositions.

Questions You Should Anticipate

  • How will you use the investment, and what are your next steps?
  • What is your timeline for development, and how do you plan to execute it?
  • How will you adapt if your initial plan faces challenges?
  • Can you describe your market opportunity and sales strategy in detail?
  • What are your plans for future funding and financial sustainability?
  • Are you prepared to revise your approach to meet market realities?

Building the Angel Financing

  • Use a SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) for early-stage funding.
  • SAFE agreements define terms of investment against future equity without maturity.
  • SAFE agreements reduce legal fees and simplify the investment process.
  • Agree on a valuation cap and potential discount for future equity.
  • SAFE eliminates default risks and interest payments, benefiting early startups.
  • Ensure all terms are clear to avoid disputes in future funding rounds.
